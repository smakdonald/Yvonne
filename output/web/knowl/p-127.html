Have teachers complete in pairs or groups. Then, choose a couple of teacher groups to share their visuals. Then, ask the class, “Choose the one most different from your own and come up with a 1-minute-or-less oral explanation using that visual. Talk it aloud to yourself.” Students may have a difficult time creating a visual, but don't let this bog down progress too much. If no student group has a suitable visual for discussion, utilize the one in the oral task below, or something similar that you create (e.g. a representation of \(5(2 + 3)\) compared to \((2 \times 3)5)\text{.}\)
