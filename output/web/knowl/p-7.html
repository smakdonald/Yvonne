Let \(a\) be a number, and let \(n\) be a positive integer. We use the special symbol “\(a^n\)" to represent the number we reach using “repeated multiplication by \(a\text{.}\)" In particular, the symbol \(a^n\) is bookkeeping notation, simply telling us how many factors of \(a\) appear in the repeated multiplication:
\begin{equation*} a^n=\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\dots \cdot a}_{n\ times} \end{equation*}
The superscript indicates the number of times \(a\) appears in the multiplication and is known as the exponent, from the Latin phrase expo ponere which means “out of place." (Michael Stifel is credited with coining the term in the 1500's.) The number \(a\) is now called the base of the expression.
