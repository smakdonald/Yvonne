Outcomes 1.6.

  • When \(n \in \mathbb{N}^+\text{,}\) we define:
    • \(a^n=\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\dots \cdot a}_{n\ times}\) as repeated multiplication by \(a\text{.}\)
  • When \(-n\in \mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) (so \(n\in \mathbb{N}\)),
    • \(a^0\) is the , so \(a^0a^x=a^x\) for any \(x\text{.}\) This means \(a^0=1\text{.}\)
    • \(a^{-1}\) as a positive or zero solution to \(ax=1\text{.}\) This means \(a^{-1}=\frac{1}{a}\text{,}\) for \(a\neq 0\text{.}\)
    • \(a^{-n}\) as a positive or zero solution to \(a^n x=1\text{.}\) This means \(a^{-n}=\frac{1}{a^n}\text{,}\) or equivalently, \((\frac{1}{a})^n\text{,}\) for \(a\neq 0\text{.}\)
  • When \(p/q \in \mathbb{Q}^+\text{,}\) where \(p, q \in \mathbb{N}^+\text{,}\) we define
    • \(a^{1/q}\) as a positive or zero solution to \(x^q=a\text{.}\) This is also known as a .
    • \(a^{p/q}=(a^{1/q})^p\text{,}\) which we take to be equivalent to \((a^p)^{1/q}\text{.}\)
  • When \(-p/q\in \mathbb{Q}\text{,}\) where \(p, q\in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) and \(q\neq 0\text{,}\)
    • \(a^{-1/q}\) as a positive or zero solution to \(a^{1/q} x=1\text{.}\) This means \(a^{-1/q}=\frac{1}{a^{1/q}}\text{,}\) or equivalently, \((\frac{1}{a})^{1/q}\text{,}\) for \(a\neq 0\text{.}\)
    • \(a^{-p/q}=(a^{-1})^{p/q}\text{,}\) which we take to be equivalent to \((a^{p/q})^{-1}\)
    Note: Such solutions, when they exist, are unique. When such a solution does not exist, we say that the value of the power does not exist.
