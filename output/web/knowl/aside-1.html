In the expression \(a^n\text{,}\) we say the base is \(a\text{,}\) the exponent is \(n\text{.}\) The entire expression \(a^n\) is called a power.
When \(n \in \mathbb{N}^+\text{,}\) we define
\begin{equation*} a^n=\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\dots \cdot a}_{n\ times} \end{equation*}
with repeated multiplication by \(a\text{.}\)
When \(n\in \mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Q}, \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) we need to extend the definition of \(a^n\text{.}\) We will do so in this chapter.
