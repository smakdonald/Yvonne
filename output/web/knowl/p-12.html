Using superscripts to indicate repeated multiplication is an ancient idea, having first been introduced in very limited form by Diophantus nearly 2,000 years ago in his so-called syncopated algebra. With the exception of using superscripts, Diophantus' notation bears little resemblance to our modern idea of exponents. (For example, he used \(\Delta^Y\) to represent “the second power of an unknown" and \(K^Y\) to represent “the cube of an unknown.")
