Definition 1.20.

Any natural number \(n\) can be thought of as location and movement, where, given an origin \(0\) and a unit distance \(1\) on the number line,
  • “\(0\)” is the movement of staying still, and its location is the origin.
  • “\(1\)” is the movement of translating \(1\) unit to the right, and the location of moving \(1\) unit to the right when starting from \(0\text{.}\)
  • “\(n\)”, for \(n>0\text{,}\) is the movement of \(n\) repeated translations of \(1\text{,}\) and the location of moving \(n\) units to the right when starting from \(0\text{.}\)
A rational number \(p/q\text{,}\) where \(p,q\in \mathbb{N}\) and \(q\neq 0\text{,}\) can be thought of as location and movement as follows:
  • \(1/q\) is the movement that you need to take \(q\) times to get from \(0\) to \(1\text{;}\) in other words, it is a solution to the equation \(qx=1\text{.}\) It is the location you get by moving this much to the right when starting from \(0\text{.}\)
  • \(p/q\) is the movement of \(p\) repeated translations of \(1/q\text{,}\) and the location of moving this much to the right when starting from \(0\text{.}\)
